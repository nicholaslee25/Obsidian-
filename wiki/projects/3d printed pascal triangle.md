## Overview

3D print a physical Pascal's triangle as a display piece. The graph structure lends itself to interesting geometric/visual representations — rows of numbers, Sierpinski triangle pattern (odd/even coloring), or a layered relief.

## Design Directions

- **Flat relief** — rows of hexagons or circles with numbers, raised from a base plate
- **Sierpinski pattern** — shade odd vs even numbers; produces a fractal triangle — visually striking
- **Layered/stacked** — each row is a separate extruded layer, stacking taller toward the apex
- **Graph nodes + edges** — print the actual graph structure with nodes connected by rods/edges

## Decisions (TBD)

- Which representation? (number grid, Sierpinski pattern, graph structure)
- Display orientation — wall-hung or freestanding?
- Size — desk piece or large wall art?
- Color — single color or multi-color to highlight patterns (e.g. multiples of 2, 3, 5)
- Material — PLA fine, PETG if near heat
