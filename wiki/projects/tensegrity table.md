## Overview

A tensegrity table held together entirely by tension cables and compression rods — the top appears to float with no rigid connections to the base. Could potentially inform the [[woodwork shelf]] design but standing alone for now.

Tensegrity works because compression members never touch each other — tension cables distribute load across the whole system. Incredibly strong-to-weight ratio.

## Materials

- Compression rods (wood dowels, metal pipe, or turned wood legs)
- Tension cable (dyneema, steel wire, or paracord)
- Table top (wood slab, acrylic, or metal plate)
- Base platform
- Turnbuckles or tensioners for adjustment
