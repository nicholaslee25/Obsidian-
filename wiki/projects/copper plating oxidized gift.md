## Overview

Copper plate an object and then oxidize/patina it for a gift. The patina gives a green-blue aged look (verdigris) or dark brown depending on the process — looks expensive, takes some chemistry.

## Process Options

### Electroplating (proper copper coating)
- Copper sulfate solution, DC power supply, copper anode, object as cathode
- Coats the object in a real copper layer
- Works on metal, conductive-painted surfaces (graphite/carbon)
- Non-conductive objects (wood, plastic) need a conductive primer first

### Oxidation / Patina Methods
After plating (or on raw copper):
- **Liver of sulfur** → dark brown/black, fast
- **Ammonia + salt vapor** → verdigris (blue-green), slower, classic aged look
- **Vinegar + salt** → similar to ammonia method, more accessible
- **Heat** → red/orange/blue tones, no chemicals

## Ideas for What to Plate

- A 3D printed object (needs conductive coating first)
- A small wooden piece
- A found object with sentimental value
- Something machined

## Materials Needed

- Copper sulfate (CuSO₄) — electroplating solution
- DC power supply or battery + wires
- Copper anode (sheet or wire)
- Conductive primer (if plating non-metal)
- Oxidizing agent (liver of sulfur, ammonia, vinegar)
- Sealant/clear coat (optional — lock in the patina)
