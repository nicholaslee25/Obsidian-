## Favorite Styles

- **Long shutter speed** — slow shutter captures motion blur while a stationary subject stays sharp. Water, traffic, light trails, stars. Needs a tripod.
- **Reflection shots** — water, glass, mirrors. Symmetry and doubled compositions.

---

## Settings (plain English)

**ISO** — sensor sensitivity to light.
- Low (100–400): clean image, needs decent light
- High (1600+): brighter in dark, adds grain/noise
- Rule: keep ISO as low as the conditions allow

**Shutter speed** — how long the sensor is exposed.
- Fast (1/500s+): freezes motion
- Slow (1/30s and below): blurs motion — the long exposure effect
- Long exposure needs: tripod + remote shutter or timer

**Aperture (f-stop)** — how wide the lens opening is.
- Low f-number (f/1.8): wide open, blurry background (bokeh), good in low light
- High f-number (f/11+): everything in focus, good for landscapes
