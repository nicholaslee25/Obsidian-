## Overview

A custom-built or custom-modified Rubik's cube — machined, printed, or hand-finished. Flagged as low plausibility: cube mechanisms require extremely tight tolerances. A 3D printed cube is doable but tends to feel rough. Machining one is a serious undertaking.

Possible middle ground: buy a cube and customize the stickers, tiles, or external case rather than building the mechanism.

## Materials

- 3D print route: resin printer (higher tolerance than FDM), springs, screws
- Machined route: aluminum or delrin stock, lathe + mill time
- Modification route: quality speedcube base + custom tiles or case
