## Chinese Culture

### Etiquette & Customs

| Custom | Meaning / Why |
|---|---|
| **Tap 2–3 fingers when someone pours your tea** | Silent "thank you" — historically a gesture of kowtowing to the emperor. Two fingers = two legs bowing; three fingers = full prostration. Still used today as a discreet thanks at the table. |
| **Leave the teapot lid slightly open (or tilted)** | Signal to the server at dim sum that you want a refill. No need to flag anyone down — they'll top you up when they pass. |
| **Don't step over a person sitting or lying down** | Superstition: stepping over someone condemns them to stop growing (stay short). Go around, always. |
