## Monitors

### Panel Types

| Type | Strengths | Weaknesses | Best for |
|---|---|---|---|
| **IPS** | Best colors, wide viewing angles | Slight backlight bleed, pricier | All-around use, creative work |
| **TN** | Fastest response time, cheapest | Washed-out colors, bad viewing angles | Competitive gaming only |
| **VA** | Best contrast (deep blacks) | Slower response, slight color ghosting | Movies, immersive gaming |
| **OLED** | Perfect blacks, best contrast + color | Burn-in risk, expensive | Premium setups, dark-room use |

### Other Monitor Specs

- **Refresh rate (Hz)** — frames per second the panel can show. 60Hz fine for general use; 144Hz+ for gaming
- **Response time (ms)** — how fast pixels change. Lower = less motion blur/ghosting
- **Resolution** — 1080p / 1440p / 4K. Higher = sharper, needs more GPU horsepower
- **HDR** — brighter highlights, darker shadows. Quality varies wildly by tier (HDR400 is basically marketing)
