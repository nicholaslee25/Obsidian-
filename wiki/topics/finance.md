## Concepts

### What are clawbacks?

A **clawback** is a contractual provision that lets a company or employer **take back money already paid** if certain conditions aren't met or are later violated.

Common in:
- **Executive compensation** — bonuses paid based on financial results that are later found to be misstated; the company claws back the bonus
- **Private equity / investment banking** — carried interest paid to fund managers can be clawed back if later returns don't meet the hurdle rate
- **Government benefits** — overpayments can be clawed back from future benefits
- **Employment contracts** — signing bonuses clawed back if you leave before a set period

Key point: it's not a penalty — it's recovering money that was paid prematurely or based on conditions that didn't hold.

### High-Yield Savings Account (HYSA) — Pros & Cons

| Pros | Cons |
|---|---|
| Higher APY than standard savings (often 4–5% vs 0.01%) | Rate is variable — can drop anytime |
| FDIC insured up to $250K | Not as liquid as checking (withdrawal limits in some cases) |
| No lock-up period (vs. CDs) | Rate still usually lower than stocks or CDs long-term |
| Easy to open online | Often at online banks — no physical branch |
| Good emergency fund vehicle | May have minimum balance requirements |

Best use: emergency fund (3–6 months expenses), short-term savings you'll need in <2 years.

---

### CDs (Certificates of Deposit)

Lock your money for a fixed term (3 months to 5 years) at a guaranteed rate. Rate is higher than HYSA but you can't touch the money without a penalty.

**Charles Schwab** — noted to have some of the best CD rates. No physical locations, but solid online platform. Worth checking when shopping CD rates.
