## What to Look For When Buying

### Location (non-negotiable, can't be fixed)
- Neighborhood trajectory — is it improving or declining?
- School district quality (affects resale even if you don't have kids)
- Flood zone, wildfire zone, earthquake zone — check FEMA maps
- Proximity to work, highways, transit, grocery, hospital
- Crime stats — local police reports, NeighborhoodScout
- HOA — does one exist? What are the rules and fees? Can they assess you?

### The Property Itself
| Thing to Check | What to Ask |
|---|---|
| **Foundation** | Cracks? Settling? Water intrusion in basement/crawl space? |
| **Roof** | Age, material, condition. Replace every 20–30 years ($10k–$30k+) |
| **HVAC** | Age and condition. Replace every 15–20 years ($5k–$15k) |
| **Electrical panel** | Breakers or fuses? Capacity? Any aluminum wiring (fire risk)? |
| **Plumbing** | Material (copper = good, galvanized = old, polybutylene = bad), any active leaks |
| **Windows** | Double-pane? Any fogging between panes (seal failure)? |
| **Water damage** | Stains on ceilings, warped floors, musty smell = red flag |
| **Attic/insulation** | Proper ventilation, no mold, adequate insulation R-value |

### Financial Due Diligence
- **Price per sq ft** — compare to neighborhood comps (sold, not listed)
- **Property taxes** — look up the current bill; ask if there are special assessments
- **Homeowner's insurance** estimate — get a quote before closing
- **HOA fees** — monthly + special assessments history
- **Closing costs** — typically 2–5% of purchase price (title, escrow, lender fees)
- **Title search** — confirm no liens, disputes, or easements that affect use
- Always get a **home inspection** ($300–$500 well spent) — use a licensed independent inspector, not one recommended by the seller's agent

### Red Flags
- Seller won't allow inspection or is rushing the close
- Recent cosmetic renovations covering structural issues
- Active permits that weren't closed out
- History of flooding/water remediation
- Evidence of mold, asbestos (pre-1980 homes), or radon
- Sewer scope — older homes should have the sewer line scoped ($150–$300); collapsed or root-invaded lines = expensive

## How to Maintain a House

### Routine Schedule

| Frequency | Task |
|---|---|
| **Monthly** | Check HVAC filter — replace if dirty (1" filters: every 1–3 months; 4" filters: every 6–12 months) |
| **Monthly** | Test smoke + CO detectors |
| **Quarterly** | Check water softener salt (if applicable) |
| **Spring** | Clean gutters, inspect roof, check caulking on windows/doors, test AC, flush water heater |
| **Fall** | Clean gutters again, reverse ceiling fans (clockwise for winter), winterize exterior faucets, check furnace |
| **Annually** | Inspect attic for moisture/pests, check foundation for new cracks, test sump pump, service HVAC |
| **As needed** | Re-caulk tub/shower (grout + caulk fail, water gets in, rot follows) |

### Big Ticket Items to Budget For
| Item | Lifespan | Rough Cost |
|---|---|---|
| Roof | 20–30 years | $8k–$30k |
| HVAC system | 15–20 years | $5k–$15k |
| Water heater | 10–15 years | $1k–$3k |
| Kitchen appliances | 10–15 years | varies |
| Windows (full replace) | 20–30 years | $10k–$25k |
| Driveway (asphalt) | 20–25 years | $3k–$10k |

**Rule of thumb:** Budget 1–2% of home value per year for maintenance and repairs.

## Being a Landlord — Renting Out a Property

### Legal Responsibilities
- Know your **state and local landlord-tenant laws** — they vary wildly (eviction rules, security deposit limits, notice requirements)
- **Lease agreement** — use a lawyer-reviewed template at minimum; spell out: rent amount, due date, late fees, lease term, pet policy, maintenance responsibilities
- **Security deposit** — most states have a cap (1–2 months rent); must be returned within a set window after move-out with itemized deductions
- **Entry notice** — most states require 24–48 hour notice before entering (emergencies excepted)
- **Fair Housing Act** — cannot discriminate based on race, religion, sex, national origin, disability, familial status

### Habitability Requirements
You are legally required to maintain:
- Functioning heat, plumbing, and electricity
- Weatherproof roof and walls
- Pest-free property
- Working smoke/CO detectors
- Safe common areas

### Tenant Screening
- Credit check (aim for 620+, but context matters)
- Background check
- Income verification (standard: 3× monthly rent)
- Rental history / references from previous landlords
- Use a consistent screening criteria for all applicants (Fair Housing)

### Preparing a Property to Rent
- Deep clean + paint (neutral colors — white/light gray)
- Fix everything — deferred maintenance becomes tenant complaints
- Document condition with dated photos before move-in
- Set rent using Zillow/Rentometer to benchmark local rates
- Decide: manage yourself vs. hire a property manager (~8–10% of rent)

### Repairs: Who Pays?
| Who's responsible | Examples |
|---|---|
| **Landlord** | HVAC failure, roof leak, plumbing issues, appliances included in lease |
| **Tenant** | Damage beyond normal wear and tear, lost keys, clogged drains they caused |
| **Gray area** | Bulbs, minor wear — set expectations in the lease |

### Eviction (Last Resort)
1. Written notice (Pay or Quit / Cure or Quit / Unconditional Quit — depends on reason)
2. File with the court if tenant doesn't comply
3. Attend hearing
4. Sheriff's lockout if court rules in your favor
Never do self-help eviction (changing locks, removing belongings) — illegal everywhere, major liability.

## House Flipping

### The Math First
- **ARV (After Repair Value)** — what the house is worth fully renovated; use comparable sold homes (comps)
- **The 70% Rule:** Don't pay more than **70% of ARV minus estimated repair costs**
  - Example: ARV = $300k, repairs = $50k → max purchase price = (300 × 0.70) − 50 = **$160k**
- Factor in: purchase price + closing costs + carrying costs (mortgage/taxes/insurance during renovation) + selling costs (agent fees ~5–6%) + profit margin

### Financing a Flip
- **Hard money loans** — short-term (6–18 months), asset-based, high interest (10–15%). Designed for flips. Fast to close.
- **Private money** — borrow from individuals, negotiated terms
- **Cash** — ideal; no interest drag
- Traditional mortgages are too slow and often won't lend on distressed properties

### Finding Deals
- Foreclosures / bank-owned (REO)
- Estate sales, probate properties
- Tax lien / tax deed sales
- Off-market (direct mail, driving for dollars, wholesalers)
- MLS — occasionally, if it's been sitting and price cut

### Renovation — ROI Hierarchy
Best return on investment:
1. **Kitchen** — counters, cabinet doors/hardware, appliances (full gut only if necessary)
2. **Bathrooms** — vanity, tile, fixtures
3. **Curb appeal** — paint, landscaping, front door
4. **Flooring** — LVP or hardwood throughout
5. **Fresh paint** — whole house, neutral tones
Avoid: pools, highly personalized upgrades, over-improving for the neighborhood

### Common Pitfalls
- Underestimating repair costs (always add 15–20% contingency)
- Timeline delays eat into carrying costs
- Buying retail, selling in a slow market
- Ignoring permit requirements (unpermitted work surfaces at inspection and kills deals)
- Emotional attachment to the project

### Exit Strategies
| Strategy | Description |
|---|---|
| **Fix & Flip** | Renovate and sell. Quick profit, taxed as ordinary income (short-term). |
| **Fix & Rent** | Renovate and hold. Slower return, builds equity and cash flow. |
| **BRRRR** | Buy, Rehab, Rent, Refinance (cash-out), Repeat. Scales a portfolio using one pool of capital. |
| **Wholesale** | Assign the contract to another buyer for a fee — no renovation needed. Fast, low margin. |

## Useful Terms

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **ARV** | After Repair Value — projected market value post-renovation |
| **Escrow** | Third party that holds funds/documents during a transaction until conditions are met |
| **Title** | Legal ownership document; title insurance protects against disputes |
| **Appraisal** | Licensed appraiser's estimate of market value (required by lenders) |
| **Earnest money** | Deposit showing you're serious; goes toward closing costs or is forfeited if you walk |
| **Contingency** | Condition in the offer that must be met (inspection, financing, appraisal) or you can exit |
| **Cap rate** | (Net operating income / Property value) × 100 — measures rental yield |
| **Cash-on-cash return** | Annual pre-tax cash flow / cash invested — measures actual return on your money |
| **Equity** | Market value minus what you owe — what you'd pocket if you sold |
