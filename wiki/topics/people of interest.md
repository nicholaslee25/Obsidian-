## Ray Kurzweil

**Who:** Engineer, inventor, and futurist. Director of Engineering at Google.

**Known for:**
- Predicting the technological singularity — the point where AI surpasses human intelligence
- The "law of accelerating returns" — technology doesn't grow linearly, it compounds
- Books: *The Singularity Is Near*, *How to Create a Mind*, *The Singularity Is Nearer*
- Inventions: flatbed scanner, text-to-speech synthesizer, optical character recognition (OCR)

**Worth engaging with if:** you're interested in AI, the future of technology, or long-term thinking.

---

## Bulwer-Lytton

**Full name:** Edward Bulwer-Lytton, 1st Baron Lytton

**Who:** Victorian-era English novelist, playwright, poet, and politician. Wrote for British colonial audiences.

**Known for:**
- Coining phrases still used today: *"the pen is mightier than the sword"*, *"the great unwashed"*, *"pursuit of the almighty dollar"*
- Opening line of *Paul Clifford* (1830): *"It was a dark and stormy night..."* — now used as a benchmark for purple prose
- The **Bulwer-Lytton Fiction Contest** exists to honor intentionally terrible opening sentences in his style
- Wrote across genres: historical fiction, science fiction (early), drama
